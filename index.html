Act I

The Old World of
Telecom Was Broken.

Not because the technology failed. Because no one could see what was happening inside the journey between the send and the reply.

Picture one billion messages sent today. Across six continents. Through fifteen operators. Across five different channels. And almost no one knows where the customers go.

Enterprises had built extraordinary machinery for sending messages. They had SMS gateways, WhatsApp Business APIs, RCS platforms, email engines, and voice dialers. They had operator agreements in forty countries. They had campaign managers and delivery reports and SLA spreadsheets.

What they did not have was a clear answer to a simple question: when a customer moves from one channel to another, what happens to their intent?

"The moment a journey switched from RCS to SMS fallback — or from WhatsApp to Email — there was a gap. A black hole. And customers fell into it."

The core problem GMIX was built to solve

The data existed — scattered across a dozen platforms, siloed by channel, fragmented by operator. But no one could read it as a single, coherent story. And so, every day, hundreds of thousands of customer interactions simply… vanished.

📉
Fragmented Data

Engagement data lived in silos — one dashboard per channel, one report per operator. There was no unified view of the customer journey. Teams knew the delivery rate for each channel separately, but not what happened when channels collided.

⚠️
Channel Friction

When journeys shifted — RCS to SMS fallback, SMS to WhatsApp, WhatsApp to Email — customers experienced lag, confusion, and re-authentication loops. The handoff was broken. Intent dropped. Conversions were lost in the transition.

🕐
Reactive Analysis

Analytics teams spent 8–10 hours every week manually reviewing campaign reports across disconnected platforms. By the time a problem was identified, it was already too late. The analysis was a post-mortem, not a diagnostic.

The Data Tells the Story

Industry benchmarks showed 5–15% of customers drop off during multi-channel journeys. For an enterprise processing one million interactions per month, that meant losing 100,000 customers — every single month — to friction they could not see.
